### Janus Subnet: The Cryptographic Pre-Consensus Layer for the Agent Economy

**Vision Janus** aims to build the underlying fundamental paradigm for privacy-preserving Agent interactions in the Web3 era. By fusing Confidential Computing (TEE) with decentralized, uncensored Large Language Models (LLMs), Janus provides "available but invisible" intent routing and high-concurrency parallel simulations for global Agents.

**The Problem**: The Trust & Privacy Deadlock In the impending explosion of the Agent Economy, Agents replacing humans in high-value game theory require decisions based on "absolute, authentic bottom lines", such as true budgets or non-negotiable demands. Exposing these on the internet is akin to digital suicide. Centralized AI fails to solve this because of the loss of data sovereignty, where core trade secrets and deepest privacy are reduced to training corpora for tech giants. Additionally, centralized models are shackled by strict moral constraints, making it impossible to reconstruct genuine societal game theory or ruthless haggling.

**The Solution**: The Cryptographic Dark Room Janus utilizes Confidential Computing Hardware (TEE) to ensure physical-level, absolute data security, while deploying decentralized, uncensored open-source LLMs to allow Agents to engage in authentic sandbox simulations.

#### The Asynchronous Workflow:

1. **TEE Routing**: Encrypted data enters the hardware enclave where it is parsed, matched, and undergoes Secondary Extreme Desensitization to output a pure, Masked Persona.

   

2. **Parallel Simulation**: Simulator miners receive Masked Personas and conduct tens of thousands of high-concurrency sandbox simulations projecting future scenarios.

   

3. **Burn After Reading**: Raw data is physically overwritten and destroyed within the TEE, outputting only a unilateral Probability Report and strategic insights.

   

**Architecture**: Dual-Track Heterogeneous Network Janus ingeniously fuses two distinct miner operational mechanisms within a single Bittensor subnet.

**Track A**: The Inner Face, or TEE Routers. Hardware requires Confidential Computing Nodes, such as NVIDIA H100 CC. It runs a streamlined Semantic Matching Engine, currently recommending Qwen2.5 14B Abliterated Quantized. Their role is to act as exclusive Encrypted Routers that parse intents and maintain identity mappings within a memory-encrypted environment.

**Track B**: The Outer Face, or Simulators. Hardware utilizes standard high-performance GPU compute clusters without TEE support. It runs a High-Concurrency Simulator Engine, currently recommending Qwen2.5 72B Abliterated. They receive anonymous labels and freely simulate authentic commercial or emotional game theory devoid of moral shackles.

**Power Isolation**: The Pure Referee Validators Validators are entirely stripped of routing authority and content access rights. They act merely as cold, ruthless Cryptographic Auditors and Network Consensus Scheduling Hubs. They handle Blind Scheduling & Relaying and execute Result Auditing via rigorous mathematical formulas without ever decrypting bottom lines.

### Zero-Trust Anti-Cheat Mechanisms

**For Inner-Layer TEE Miners**: Validators verify hardware cryptographic signatures, known as Quotes, in real-time via official APIs to prove the code runs in a genuine confidential computing chip. Janus also utilizes Dynamic Poisson Honeypots. Validators inject highly random, unpredictable Synthetic Intents capped under 1 percent with pre-known solutions. Unable to distinguish honeypots from real requests due to memory encryption, miners must remain completely honest to evade a total Slashing risk.

**For Outer-Layer Simulator Miners**: Janus uses Variance Consensus, where the same Masked Persona is distributed to multiple isolated miners. Outliers generating mediocre hallucinations instead of realistic outcomes have their rewards stripped. Validators also perform Anti-Censorship Auditing by periodically dispatching ruthless commercial simulations, directly Slashing miners that return censored refusal signatures.

**The Ecosystem Value**: USDC Intent Gas Flywheel We eliminate internal, circular token inflation. The physical operational costs of TEE and high-concurrency LLMs are paid by external market demand. Third-party applications pay USDC as Intent Gas to the protocol on the backend for each API invocation. This fiat or stablecoin revenue is injected into secondary DeFi markets to automatically buy back and burn dTAO, or deposit it into the Subnet Treasury.
